Research

My research lies at the intersection of behavioral economics, law, and institutional design. I use controlled experiments to study how people interpret and comply with formal rules.

Working Papers

Rule Text vs. Rule Purpose: Textualism and Purposivism in the Lab

Smith, J. & Coauthor, A. (2025)

We study how participants respond when the literal text of a rule diverges from the rule's underlying purpose. Using a novel coordination experiment with six conditions we find that most participants follow the text even when doing so violates the purpose.

Working Paper (PDF) Pre-registration

Anticipated Punishment and Rule Compliance

Smith, J. & Coauthor, B. (2025)

We add a third-party reviewer to the rule-following experiment and examine how the anticipation of punishment shapes compliance decisions. Participants are more purposivist when their choices are observable.

Working Paper (PDF)

Work in Progress

Social Norms of Rule Interpretation

Smith, J., Coauthor, A. & Coauthor, B.

What do people think others should do when a rule's text and purpose diverge? We elicit normative judgments using Likert scales and deduction-point tasks across all six divergence conditions.
